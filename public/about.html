Leader from the Founder

The Arab World is going through transitional times. Some call it the Arab Spring. Others call it Arab awakening, and few call it destructive chaos. However, they all agree that the Arab world will never be the same again.

If there is a lesson to be learn from Arab revolutions, its that we as societies should have a vision of what we aspire to become. We shall all engage in a civil discussion to identify our priorities that is in line with our value systems.

The politicians must not drive the conversation; we as Arabs youth should.

The youth of the region represent the major population of the Arab world. IN today’s digital world, we have a golden opportunity to connect, converse, and leverage each other, for the sake of better future,

Arab Visions 2025 is an attempt to create a space of discussion and looking forward in a structured and inclusive manner. Our objective is to attract the Arab youth to come together and engage in a dialogue, create a battle of ideas and let the best ideas win.

We want to envision the social, political, economic, sports, spiritual ….etc futures of every Arab country.

This is a space to shape our ideas all together and aspire to the ones that we like the most.

We are on the same boat. We might agree or disagree on many things and that is ok. What is more important is to have a direction, and have a vision.

Guidelines

Our Promise:
promote innovate ideas for debates give arab youth’s voices coverage expose the best ideas from arab youths to influential policy makers and leaders of the arab world

+ Collaboration: everyone has something valuable to contribute, that by building on each other’s ideas we can achieve far more than any one of us could on our own, that each of us has a role to play in our community.
- To foster an environment conducive to collaboration
- Keep your comments concise and precise; it is much easier to engage others that way
- Constructive engagement
Present alternative – visioning, constructive debate Good comment that opposes a view should include suggestion for how to do it better

+ Respect
- Consider others’ points of views by giving your full attention to these opposing points of views. All ideas deserve serious consideration
- No personal attacks

+ Attribution
- Avoid ad hominem ideas – present an alternative
- Remove comments that do not observed the values espoused in this principle

This shared respect will promote open and honest conversations and the insights they make possible. Some ideas are associated with one person, while others are group efforts. We endeavor to recognize, credit, and respect the rights and interests of the creators.
